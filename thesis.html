THE THESIS Act 4 · part 1 · the thesis

Why I'm betting on Strats. And how I'm sizing the bet.

My thesis has always focused on four things:

  1. A protocol I will personally use. Returns that accelerate the more I'm right, no-liquidation leverage, and real yield on any token. These are products I actually want in my portfolio.
  2. Proven PMF or a clear path to growth. Peapods hit $105M TVL in 8 months with one narrow product, earning DeFi-leading revenue efficiency. Strats is that same engine, dramatically improved.
  3. A trustworthy team with a track record. Self-funded audits. Shipped through two bear markets. Never pivoted to whatever narrative trended. Years of consistent execution.
  4. A token that directly benefits from protocol usage. 60% of every dollar of revenue routes to $STRATS buybacks. Not governance votes. Not future fee switches. Revenue flows to holders by design, from day one.

StratsFi checks all four. But what makes this specific: the timing. Peapods' cascade was a leverage failure, not a revenue failure. Strats fixes the leverage layer. At $4.8M market cap, the market hasn't re-priced for the relaunch yet.

Part 2 estimates how much TVL Strats can realistically capture over 24 months. Part 3 converts that TVL into what it means for the token price.

Act 4 · part 3 · the estimated valuation

The valuation follows from performance, not narrative.

Most crypto valuations are vibes. This one has a measurable chain: TVL generates revenue, 60% buys back the token, and the remaining 40% funds growth that drives more TVL, which generates more revenue, which drives more buybacks. We can benchmark what that cashflow is worth by comparing with other DeFi protocols that share revenue with token holders.

The model: TVL × 0.5% rev/TVL × 12 × 60% × 25× P/E. Simplified: FDV = TVL × 0.9. You can disagree with any input. But the logic is transparent, every number is sourced, and the formula is the same either way.

Even the bearish case (Peapods' proven rate, nothing improves) produces a 29× from today's price. The conservative case alone is 104×. Downside risks (exploits, slower growth, competitive pressure) are real but observable within months of launch.

The coin flip you'd always take. Put in $100. There's a ~20% chance something breaks and you lose it. An ~80% chance the protocol works and you win somewhere between $2,900 (bearish) and $63,600 (bullish). Every $1 risked has a weighted payoff of roughly $50-$100. Most investments in crypto, venture, or public equity don't offer that ratio.

Compare to anything else you could buy. ETH from here might 2-3× in a bull cycle. BTC might 2×. A narrative altcoin might 10×. A pre-launch protocol with a proven revenue engine and a $4.8M market cap has a 29× floor before any improvement kicks in. The risk/reward is structurally different.

The risks are real but monitorable. Smart contract exploits, team execution failure, competitive pressure, revenue underperformance. All legitimate. But each is observable within weeks to months of launch. If month-3 TVL is tracking bearish or below, you have data to act on. If it's tracking conservative or above, the thesis is validating in real time.
